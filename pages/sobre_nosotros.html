Sobre nosotros

El International Cognitive Research Consortium (ICRC) es un grupo internacional de investigación en temas aplicados de ciencias cognitivas, del comportamiento y de la educación, que busca encontrar soluciones a diversos interrogantes en el estudio delas ciencias de la conducta y de aspectos cognitivos de los procesos del aprendizaje.

Además, el ICRC está comprometido a introducir el uso de métodos de machine-learning en sus áreas de investigación, y aplicarlos en temas de cognición, aprendizaje, evaluación, seguridad nacional y salud.

El ICRC está integrado por un equipo interdisciplinario de científicos investigadores y docentes universitarios de diversas instituciones en Europa, USA y América Latina, como así también estudiantes avanzados y estudiantes de doctorado, interesados en estudiar procesos cognitivos y de autorregulación y sus efectos sobre el comportamiento y el aprendizaje. El grupo propone una amplia agenda de investigación y participación en publicaciones científicas, de divulgación y conferencias internacionales.

Directores

Mariel F. Musso

PhD in Psychology (University of San Luis, Argentina, 2007) and PhD in Educational Sciences (KU Leuven University, Belgium, 2016). She was a Research Fellow at the National Council for Scientific and Technical Research (CONICET). Dr. Musso has been an international consultant in many national assessment projects for Assessment Group International and the World Bank, in countries of Southeast Asia, Latin America, Middle East and Europe. She is currently an Associate Researcher at CONICET, Argentina, since 2015, and Professor in the Department of Psychology at UADE University, Argentina, since 2010. She has published many papers in journals, several book chapters, and she is a member of various international professional associations with regular participation in their conferences (European Association for Research on Learning and Instruction, Association for Educational Assessment- Europe, American Educational Research Association, National Council on Measurements in Education, International Congress of Psychology, International Congress of Applied Psychology, and Interamerican Congress of Psychology). She coordinates several research projects at CONICET and at UADE University, in her main areas of interest: self-regulated learning, modeling of cognitive processes, machine-learning methods in education, and new methods in educational assessment.

Eduardo Cascallar

Dr. Eduardo Cascallar received his Ph.D. degree from the University of Texas at Austin. He currently is Special Guest Professor at the University of Leuven, Belgium, and Managing Director for Assessment Group International (Belgium/USA). He is also an international consultant for the World Bank, OECD and UNESCO. Dr. Cascallar is also member of the International Consulting Group advising Chile on topics related to the PSU university entrance examinations. He was a technical expert (Belgium) for the AHELO project of OECD for the assessment of higher education outcomes, while also being part of OECD/World Bank country-review teams. Most recently he was Principal Research Scientist at the American Institutes for Research, working in international assessment projects, assessment of higher education examinations, and in automated predictive methodologies in national security applications. Prior to this, he was Dean of Assessment at Regents (Excelsior) College, leading an international computer-delivered testing program in higher education. Previously, he served as Director of Assessment at the City University of New York and as Coordinator for Testing and Research at the Center for the Advancement of Language Learning in Washington, for the US government, and was in the faculty of Georgetown University. Dr. Cascallar was also Research Scientist at Educational Testing Service (ETS) in the Division of Cognitive and Instructional Science. Previously, he was Research Scientist in the Department of Psychology at UCLA. His research experience includes work on the assessment of cognitive abilities, the role of working memory and executive attention in academic outcomes and in complex problem solving, the role of heritability in cognitive processing levels, educational assessments, performance assessment, and large scale testing, validity and reliability, IRT applications, computer based assessment, and cognitive processing and cognitive memory modelling. More recently he has worked in various predictive methodologies in educational, medical, and national security applications. Dr. Cascallar has a long list of publications and presentations at international conferences, and is active in various international professional organizations, providing consulting services to academic and corporate institutions, and to international governmental and private organizations in Europe, USA, Canada, Middle East, Australia, and Latin America. Among these activities are current ongoing long-term projects in Kuwait, Vietnam, Thailand, Pakistan, Argentina, Kosovo, Chile, Belgium, Netherlands, USA, and Romania.
